ACU BESS Degree Alignment — How US Programs Map to Olivier's Curriculum

Olivier's ACU Bachelor of Exercise and Sports Science (BESS) has 16 specified units. Each US program is rated on how many of those units have a direct equivalent. A higher alignment score means less need to take extra courses and stronger WES credit recognition potential.

Full alignment (14–16/16 units)

UF (APK), Indiana, PBA, Barry, Virginia, USF. These programs cover anatomy, exercise physiology, biomechanics, motor control, resistance training, sport psychology, research methods, and internship — all matching Olivier's ACU units directly.

Strong alignment (10–13/16 units)

UCLA, UCSB, Wake Forest, SMU, FIU, St Edward's, Chapman, Cal State LA, Lynn, Keiser. Core exercise science covered but motor development and/or motor control may be split across courses.

Partial alignment (6–9/16 units)

Princeton, Yale, Charleston, St John's, Oklahoma City. Core science present but the degree structure differs significantly — pre-health biology pathway rather than a dedicated exercise science curriculum.

What is Athletic Scholarship?

In D1 and D2, coaches have a scholarship "pool" (9.9 equivalencies for D1 soccer; 9.0 for D2). They split this across their roster. A "full athletic scholarship" covers tuition, room, board, and fees. In practice, international players often receive 25–75% athletic aid. NAIA has no cap. D3 and Ivy League = zero athletic scholarships.

What is Academic Scholarship?

Separate from athletic aid — awarded for GPA, SAT/ACT, and academic achievement. Can stack on top of athletic scholarships. For Olivier at GPA 2.8, academic merit aid is limited but available at some schools (typically requires 3.0+). As GPA improves, more academic aid becomes accessible.

What are Living Expenses?

Beyond tuition, room & board: flights home (2× per year ~$3,000–4,000 AUD), personal spending, phone, books, health insurance. These are NOT covered by scholarships and must be budgeted separately. Platform Sports Management should clarify what exactly each scholarship covers (tuition-only vs full COA).

AUD vs USD

All school costs are in USD. The AUD amount fluctuates with exchange rates — budgeting in AUD requires a buffer. A $30,000 USD annual cost becomes ~$46,500 AUD at current rates. Use the exchange rate slider to see impact of rate changes on your budget.
